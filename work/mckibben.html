Dynamic Modeling and Control of McKibben Actuators

Background

McKibben actuators are pneumatic actuators that contract when pressurized. They were originally designed to be used as "air muscles," and indeed have some similarities with muscular tissue. Their contraction is highly nonlinear, as contraction rate decreases with absolute pressure and muscles exhibits elastic properties.

The Project

This project involved the modeling of McKibben actuators, with the purpose of attempting and evaluating control methods. Based on prior work and data, I modeled the behavior of McKibben actuators; here, we can see the effect of pressurization and depressurization.


McKibben actuator open loop behavior. Pressurization demonstrates nonlinearities in contraction, and depressurization demonstrates anisotropy.

In addition, I worked with other students to implement and evaluate control methods. For example, here we attempt position control on a periodic (sinusoid) target:


McKibben actuator position control with a periodic target. The PD controller both leads and lags the target (depending on phase), and actuator limits prevent perfect results.

Current Status and Future Work

The goal of this project was to demonstrate the limits of McKibben actuators. In general, other actuators (motors, in particular) perform better in robotics tasks, with fewer control challenges. For specific applications, McKibbens can still be the correct choice, as done in certain manipulation domains, or for robots designed for deployment in radioactive environments.
